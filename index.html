Bruce Lee

Bruce Lee was a Hong Kong-American martial artist, actor, and filmmaker.

Here's a timeline of Bruce Lee's life:

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco, California.
  • 1941 - Moved to Hong Kong with his family.
  • 1958 - Began studying martial arts under the guidance of Yip Man.
  • 1958 - Moved to the United States to attend the University of Washington.
  • 1964 - Opened his first martial arts school, the Jun Fan Gung Fu Institute, in Seattle.
  • 1971 - Starred in the TV series "The Green Hornet."
  • 1972 - Starred in "The Way of the Dragon," which he also wrote and directed.
  • 1973 - Starred in "Enter the Dragon," which became a major international success.
  • 1973 - Passed away on July 20, 1973, at the age of 32.
